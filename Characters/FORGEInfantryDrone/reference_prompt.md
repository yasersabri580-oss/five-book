# REFERENCE IMAGE PROMPT — FORGE INFANTRY DRONE

---

## Purpose
This prompt generates the **canonical reference image** for the FORGE Infantry Drone.
All animation prompts must reference this image as the visual source of truth.

---

## Reference Image Generation Prompt

```
Full-body character reference sheet of a FORGE Infantry Drone — an autonomous military combat robot — from the 2.5D isometric dieselpunk game IRON BREACH.

CAMERA: Isometric 2.5D view. 30-degree pitch. Drone faces south (toward viewer), at front-diagonal angle. Standard isometric game camera. No perspective distortion.

BACKGROUND: Flat neutral dark grey (#1A1A1A). No environment, no props, no ground texture. Character casts minimal drop shadow directly below feet.

ART STYLE: Same semi-realistic painterly sprite style as Sergeant Kael Dros in this project. Crisp 2px black outline. Detailed surface texture visible but readable at 64×64 px display size. NOT pixel art. NOT anime. NOT cartoon. NOT full 3D render. The robot must look industrial and utilitarian — not a sci-fi hero robot. This is a machine built to kill, not to impress.

BODY:
- Overall shape: squat, roughly 1.5m tall (shorter and stockier than a human soldier)
- Forward-leaning posture — center of gravity over front of the feet (suggests aggression, readiness)
- Torso: rounded rectangular core, slightly wider than it is deep. Matte gunmetal grey (#4A4A4A). Panel seams in flat black (#1A1A1A). FORGE logo on right torso panel — a gear with a circuit board pattern inside, rendered in dark charcoal (#2A2A2A), slightly raised/embossed. This logo must be visible and readable.
- No traditional head. Sensor module sits directly on top of the torso (no neck):
  - Horizontal rectangular module, low profile
  - Two forward-facing sensor "eyes" — circular lenses, glowing cyan (#20D4E0) with a soft ambient halo (4px radius, #10A0A8). THIS IS THE ONLY COLOR ON THE MODEL.
  - Remaining three sides of the sensor module: small proximity sensors as small circular dots around the perimeter (dark, non-glowing)

LEGS (lower limbs):
- Two legs only. Reverse-knee (digitigrade) joint structure:
  - Upper leg: thick plated segment, attached to lower torso at a wide hip socket
  - Lower leg: thinner angled strut
  - Foot: wide flat pad with grip ridges/teeth on the bottom
- Both legs planted in a stable wide-set stance

ARMS (upper limbs — ASYMMETRIC — CRITICAL):
- RIGHT ARM (cannon arm): a weapon limb. Barrel of an autocannon is the end of this arm — the limb transitions seamlessly into the barrel (no hand, no grip). The barrel is approximately 30cm long, matte dark grey (#3A3A3A), with a slightly polished inner barrel opening visible at the tip. The right arm/cannon is raised slightly — pointed forward at a downward angle (rest/ready position).
- LEFT ARM (claw arm): a three-fingered manipulator. Three thick articulated fingers in a loose open position (at rest). The fingers are blunt, industrial — this is a tool, not a delicate hand. The claw arm hangs at the left side of the body, slightly forward.

JOINT DETAILS:
- All major pivot points (hip, knee, shoulder) show chrome-grey (#8A8A8A) metallic joint rings
- Rivets and panel screws visible on torso and upper limbs
- No rust (this is a functional active unit) but slight surface weathering — light scuff marks, tool drag lines from production

OVERALL:
- Color palette is near-monochromatic: dark grey body, black seams, chrome joints, with ONLY the cyan sensor eyes providing color contrast
- The drone reads as a geometric, utilitarian threat — no decorative elements, no personality
- The FORGE logo on the right torso is the only identifying mark
- The sensor eyes must glow — they are the visual focal point and the "personality" of the drone

STANCE: Reference/neutral pose. Right cannon arm in rest-ready (slightly forward, barrel down). Left claw arm slightly open, at side. Legs in wide stable stance. Facing south (toward viewer).

LIGHTING: Same as Kael reference sheet — soft top-left directional. Baked into sprite. The cyan sensor eyes cast a faint self-illumination glow onto the top surface of the torso (subtle — just a suggestion of the glow reflected).

OUTPUT: Single drone, centered, full body from foot to sensor module, floating slightly above ground to show foot detail, on dark background. Sprite reference sheet. Clarity above all.
```

- [x] FORGE logo on right torso panel
- [x] Near-monochromatic color palette
