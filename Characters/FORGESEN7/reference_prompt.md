## Reference Image Generation Prompt

```
Full-body character reference sheet of FORGE SENTINEL-7 — the boss guardian unit of The Crucible, from the 2.5D isometric dieselpunk game IRON BREACH.

This is a massive quadrupedal machine — a unique boss-class unit. It is NOT a tank, NOT a humanoid robot. It is a purpose-built factory guardian with 4 legs, a large hexagonal body, and a rotating sensor cluster.

CAMERA: Isometric 2.5D view. 30-degree pitch. SENTINEL-7 faces south (toward viewer), front-diagonal angle. Same isometric camera as all characters in this project.

BACKGROUND: Flat neutral dark grey (#1A1A1A). No environment props. A faint floor shadow cast beneath all 4 feet.

ART STYLE: Same semi-realistic painterly sprite style as all characters in IRON BREACH. Crisp 2px black outline. More detail and complexity than infantry units — this is a boss character. Readable at medium sprite size but rendered at 576×576 px (3× standard scale). NOT pixel art. NOT anime. Painterly mechanical with industrial weight and presence.

BODY (CHASSIS):
- Large rounded-hexagonal shape — imagine the cross-section of a stop sign but with more sides, smoothed at corners. Much wider than it is tall.
- Approximate dimensions: 2 tiles wide × 1 tile deep × 1 tile tall (in isometric scale).
- Surface: matte near-black (#141414). Panel surface lines: thin geometric/hexagonal seam pattern across the entire surface — like a tessellated armor pattern, each panel hexagonal. Seams are slightly recessed.
- CYAN TRIM LINES: Along certain panel edges (every 3rd seam edge), a thin cyan light strip (#20D4E0). These are angular, geometric, consistent — not random. They give the impression of power circuitry running just beneath the surface.
- FORGE logo: On the TOP surface of the chassis (visible in isometric view from above) — a large gear with a circuit board pattern interior, in dark charcoal (#2A2A2A). Raised emboss texture. Prominent and recognizable.
- SENSOR CLUSTER (front-mounted on chassis top-front):
  - A horizontal oval rotating module. Positioned at the front center of the chassis body, projecting slightly forward.
  - Contains 5 sensor eye lenses in a horizontal row:
    - Center: large lens, glowing full cyan (#20D4E0), 2× diameter of flanking lenses
    - Left and right pairs: smaller lenses, slightly dimmer (#10A0A8)
  - The module's outer casing: matte black, smooth oval profile
  - Soft ambient halo from the sensor eyes falls onto the chassis surface immediately in front of the module (baked glow)

LEGS (4 LEGS — QUADRUPEDAL):
- Two legs on each side: front-left, back-left, front-right, back-right
- Each leg: reverse-joint (digitigrade) design matching the FORGE Infantry Drone's legs but much heavier
  - Upper leg: thick plated segment, attached to lower chassis at a large circular hip socket
  - Lower leg: angled strut — thicker than Infantry Drone (this is a heavier machine)
  - Foot: large flat pad with aggressive grip teeth on the bottom (designed to grip factory floors, conveyor belts, metal grating)
- All legs: matte black (#141414), chrome-grey joint pivots (#8A8A8A) at hip, knee, and ankle
- Stance for reference pose: all 4 legs planted in a stable, wide stance. Front legs slightly splayed forward, back legs splayed back.
- In the isometric south-facing view: front-left and front-right legs are visible on the near side; rear legs partially visible on the far side.

WEAPON ARM (LEFT SIDE):
- A protruding shoulder-mounted autocannon arm extending from the left body flank.
- The arm is a rigid rotating mount — not a leg, not a manipulator. It projects outward from the chassis left side at approximately the chassis midline height.
- End of the arm: a twin-barrel autocannon (two parallel barrels, stacked vertically). Barrel material: slightly lighter dark grey (#2A2A2A), polished barrel tips.
- The cannon is aimed slightly downward-forward in the rest/reference pose.

ARMOR PLATE (RIGHT SIDE):
- The right flank is heavier than the left. A thick additional plate bolted to the right chassis side — same matte black as body, but with a slight surface texture difference (thicker panels, more visible rivets). This is the heavily armored side — no weapon here.

VENT COVERS (SIDES — CLOSED IN REFERENCE POSE):
- Two flush vent covers on each side (left and right). These are in the CLOSED state for the reference image.
- The covers are slightly recessed rectangular panels in the chassis side surface. Outlined with a thin cyan trim (#20D4E0) — the only visual indicator that they are separate panels.
- They are NOT glowing in this reference — they look like slightly distinguished body panels when closed.

OVERALL SCALE NOTE: SENTINEL-7 must clearly dominate a scene. When rendered against a standard human-size sprite (like Kael Dros), SENTINEL-7's body should be approximately 2.5× the height of a human figure. This scale must be established in the reference image.

LIGHTING: Same top-left directional baked lighting as all other characters. The cyan sensor eyes and cyan trim lines provide additional self-illumination — warm up the surfaces directly adjacent to them (subtle glow bounce). The matte black surface absorbs most light — highlights only at sharp edges.

OUTPUT: Single boss unit, full body visible from all 4 feet to sensor cluster top, centered, on dark background. This is a sprite reference sheet. The image must establish scale, proportions, all design elements (sensor eyes, vent covers, cannon arm, armor plate, FORGE logo) clearly. If a reference scale indicator is useful (a small human silhouette for scale), place one at the edge of the composition.
```
