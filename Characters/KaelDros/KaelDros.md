# CHARACTER PROFILE — SERGEANT KAEL DROS

---

## Character Name
**Sergeant Kael Dros**

---

## Role in the Game
**Main playable protagonist.**
Kael is the only character the player controls throughout Chapter 1. He functions as an infantry operative and tank commander. All player abilities, upgrades, and actions are channeled through Kael.

---

## Visual Identity
Kael looks like a soldier who has been surviving in a war zone for months, not weeks. He is not clean, not pristine, not uniformed. Every piece of gear shows wear.

- **Helmet:** Military-issue cracked visor helmet. The visor is partially shattered on the right side — repaired with industrial adhesive tape. The visor tint is amber/dark gold, scratched. The helmet shell is matte olive-green with paint chips and scorch marks.
- **Torso:** Layered olive drab fatigue jacket, chest rig with visible ammo pouches, worn canvas straps. Jacket sleeves rolled to mid-forearm on the right arm. No insignia on the chest — rank patches have been torn off.
- **Left Arm:** Fully mechanical — matte gunmetal grey steel arm from shoulder to fingertips. Exposed servo joints at the elbow. Wrist panel with a small glowing interface screen (soft blue). Fingers end in articulated metal pads. Used for hacking terminals. This is the most visually distinctive feature of Kael.
- **Right Arm:** Human, weathered, scarred at the wrist.
- **Legs:** Military cargo trousers, worn and patched at the left knee. Knee pads on both legs. Heavy tactical boots with reinforced toecaps.
- **Accessories:** Utility belt with two side pouches (left: EMP charge housing; right: grenade clamp). Small radio pack clipped to back of chest rig, visible antenna stub.

---

## Personality
Quiet, efficient, and controlled under pressure. Kael processes grief through action — he does not linger or mourn aloud. He communicates in short sentences, tactical observations, and dry dark humor. He trusts no faction and relies only on Rina's intelligence and his own judgment. Beneath the control is a man who blames himself for surviving when his regiment did not.

---

## Story Relevance
Kael is the central narrative engine of Chapter 1. He is:
- The sole operative of The Rift in Verak
- The discoverer of FORGE's true origin (Conquest Protocol)
- The one who transmits the evidence that collapses SENTINEL's cover-up
- The survivor who loses his closest ally (Rina) to SENTINEL as the chapter ends

His journey is: **Lone operative → unwilling truth-seeker → isolated witness to a larger conspiracy.**

---

## Gameplay Purpose
- Player avatar for all 10 missions
- Performs all on-foot actions: movement, combat, hacking, item pickup
- Pilots all commandeered tanks
- The visual anchor for all animated sprites in the game

## Movement Style
- Quick, low-profile movement. Slight forward lean when running.
- On foot: fluid, slightly hunched. Never upright and relaxed — always ready.
- Crouched: slow, deliberate, careful steps.
- Rolling: sharp directional dive, tuck-and-roll, spring back up.
- Vaulting: one-handed push over obstacle with right arm, brief air moment, land in stride.
- In tank: only visible as hatch entry/exit animations and cockpit top-down silhouette.

---

## Combat Style
- Mid-range assault rifle as primary — controlled bursts, not wild spraying.
- Sidearm as backup — two-handed grip, point-shooting stance.
- Grenades: cooked throw — pull pin, hold, release.
- Melee: mechanical left arm strikes — short forward hook, stun-focused, not kill-focused.
- EMP: one-handed toss of EMP device (left hand).
- Hacking: press left mechanical arm against terminal — blue glow emanates from palm/wrist.

## Animation Requirements
All animations must be directional (8 directions), loopable where appropriate, and consistent in silhouette, proportions, and costume with the reference image.

| State | Loop | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Idle | Yes | Subtle breathing, slight sway, left arm micro-adjustment |
| Idle (facing up) | Yes | Back visible, helmet rear, mechanical arm on left side |
| Idle (facing down) | Yes | Full front view, visor catch light, chest rig visible |
| Idle (facing left) | Yes | Right profile, right arm visible |
| Idle (facing right) | Yes | Left profile, mechanical arm dominant |
| Walk | Yes | 6-frame cycle minimum, rifle in low-ready |
| Run | Yes | Rifle held diagonally, forward lean, dynamic |
| Crouch idle | Yes | Lowered silhouette, rifle pointed forward |
| Crouch walk | Yes | Slow, careful, compressed silhouette |
| Roll (dodge) | No | 4-frame rapid dive in direction |
| Vault | No | One-hand push over obstacle, 3-frame |
| Attack — rifle fire | Yes (while held) | Rifle raised, recoil jolt, muzzle flash position |
| Attack — pistol fire | Yes (while held) | Two-hand grip, smaller recoil |
| Attack — grenade throw | No | Cook pose → overhead arc throw |
| Attack — melee | No | Left arm hook forward, impact frame |
| Attack — EMP toss | No | Left arm underhand toss |

| Death | No | Collapse forward or backward depending on direction hit |
| Jump / Enter tank | No | One-handed pull-up animation, 1.2s |
| Exit tank | No | Drop down from hatch, land in crouch |

## Direction Requirements
All major states (Idle, Walk, Run, Attack, Hurt, Death) require **8 directions:**
- Down (toward camera)
- Up (away from camera)
- Left
- Right
- Diagonal down-left
- Diagonal down-right
- Diagonal up-left
- Diagonal up-right

Simpler states (Roll, Vault, Hack, EMP, Grenade) require **4 directions** (Down, Up, Left, Right).

---

## Technical Constraints for Consistency
- All sprites must be rendered or generated at **192×192 px per frame** (3x upscaled to 576×576 for editor preview).
- Isometric camera angle: **30-degree pitch, Southwest-to-Northeast grid orientation.**
- All directional sets must maintain identical silhouette height.
- The mechanical left arm must always be visually distinguishable from the right arm in every direction.
- The cracked visor must always show the crack on the right side relative to the character's own perspective.
- The amber visor tint must always have a subtle catch-light or glint.
- Color palette must not shift between animation states — lighting is baked into sprite, no runtime tinting.
- Death animation final frame must be held (no loop).
- All animations that will loop must have identical first and last frame silhouette to allow seamless stitching.
