# ANIMATION PROMPTS — RECLAIMER MILITIA SOLDIER — DEATH

---

## Instructions for All Death Prompts
- Use the canonical reference image (see `reference_prompt.md`) as the visual source of truth.
- **Only the fall direction changes. Costume, helmet, rubber shoulder armor, bandoliers, and weapon are strictly locked.**
- Death animation: 4-frame sequence — stagger → fall → impact → final rest (held, no loop).
- Reclaimer death is more dramatic than SENTINEL — limbs spread, body twists, less rigid collapse.
- Rubber shoulder armor may shift slightly from the fall but remains attached.
- Rifle falls away from the body (dropped further from body than SENTINEL).
- Final rest frame is permanently held on screen.
- Sprite size: 192×192 px per frame. Isometric 2.5D. 30-degree pitch.

---

## DEATH — FALL FORWARD (Hit from rear)

```
Isometric 2.5D death animation final rest frame of a Reclaimer Militia Soldier, fallen forward (face-down). Hit from behind.

Preserve exactly from reference: brown spray-painted helmet, olive jacket, rubber left shoulder armor (still attached, slightly shifted), bandoliers crossing back visible from above, cargo trousers, right boot with duct tape, hip pouch on left.

Final pose: Face-down collapse. Body spread forward on the ground in isometric view. Brown helmet pressed face-down (top and rear of helmet visible from above). Arms somewhat spread — right arm extended to the side (more spread than SENTINEL), left arm partially under torso or spread out. Rubber shoulder armor resting against the ground on the left side — slightly tilted from the fall. Bandoliers visible crossing the jacket back. Rifle fallen away from the body — not held, 1–2 tiles away to the right. Right boot duct tape visible from above. Overall: more sprawling, less rigid collapse than SENTINEL.

Background: Transparent PNG.
Art style: Semi-realistic painterly sprite, 2px black outline.
Note: Held final frame — completely still.
```
